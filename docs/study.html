Which loop strategy trains most stably?

A controlled toy-scale ablation on a single GPU: 9 loop/recurrent Transformer strategies, 4 learning rates, 3 seeds — 108 runs under one fair-comparison contract. The question isn't just “which is most accurate,” but which trains reliably.

Setup

Everything held identical across strategies; only the loop mechanism changes.

Task: modular cumulative sum — predict (Σ inputs[0..t]) mod 7 at every position. A long associative scan that rewards depth/iteration and doesn't saturate at toy scale, so strategies actually separate. Same d=128, 6 layers (or 1 weight-tied block looped), seqlen 48, batch 256, AdamW, 1500 steps, same data stream. LRs {1e-3, 3e-3, 1e-2, 3e-2} (an aggressive LR included to stress stability) × 3 seeds.

What it shows — and doesn't

Ours beats the single-pass baseline on mean accuracy (0.455 vs 0.403) and is the most stable strategy overall (σ=0.020) — and the ablation pins the cause on the near-zero-gate warm start. Gradient-truncating loops (truncated-BPTT, DEQ-style) fail to learn the scan — the documented truncation-bias failure mode. Full-BPTT recurrence (ut2) reaches a higher mean (0.74) but is spiky (grad-max p90 107) and seed-sensitive (σ=0.220).

Honest caveat. On this pure associative scan, ours does not reach the top mean — deep full-BPTT recurrence does, since a scan is its home turf. Our design's headline quality win was measured on language modeling (the d24 run: +9% CORE), not here. This toy study isolates stability (and shows ours > baseline in mean too), which is task-transferable and where ours clearly leads. Single task, single scale, 3 seeds — directional, not definitive.
